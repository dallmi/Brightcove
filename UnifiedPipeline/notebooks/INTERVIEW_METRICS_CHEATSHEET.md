## Top 10 Most Impressive Metrics

### 1. **Scale & Coverage**
```
"Analyzed X videos across Y channels, tracking Z million views"
```
**Why it matters:** Shows you can handle large-scale data

**Where to find:** Section 0 (Setup) or Section 10 (ROI Summary)

---

### 2. **Engagement Improvement**
```
"Increased completion rate from X% to Y% (+Z percentage points)"
```
**Why it matters:** Quantifiable performance improvement

**Where to find:** Section 2 (Training Effectiveness), Section 5 (Content Length)

**Example:** "Increased training completion from 31% to 76% by optimizing video length"

---

### 3. **Cost Reduction**
```
"Reduced costs by $X annually" or "Saved Y hours per month"
```
**Why it matters:** Direct ROI, speaks CFO language

**Where to find:**
- Section 4 (Channel Rationalization) - platform consolidation
- Section 7 (Archive Strategy) - storage savings

**Example:** "Consolidated underperforming channels, reducing platform costs by 23%"

---

### 4. **Mobile Growth Trend**
```
"Mobile viewing grew from X% to Y%, a Z% increase"
```
**Why it matters:** Shows you identify and act on trends

**Where to find:** Section 6 (Mobile Strategy)

**Example:** "Mobile viewing grew from 18% to 31% YoY, justifying mobile-first investment"

---

### 5. **Channel Performance Variance**
```
"Top channel achieved X% higher engagement than average"
```
**Why it matters:** Identifies optimization opportunities

**Where to find:** Section 1 (Executive Comms), Section 4 (Channel Rationalization)

**Example:** "Research channel showed 88% engagement vs. 64% averageâ€”opportunity to scale"

---

### 6. **Duration Sweet Spot**
```
"Videos X-Y minutes achieved Z% completion vs. W% for longer content"
```
**Why it matters:** Actionable content strategy insight

**Where to find:** Section 2 (Training), Section 5 (Content Length)

**Example:** "5-7 min videos: 68% completion vs. 23% for videos over 15 minutes"

---

### 7. **Drop-off Point**
```
"X% of viewers dropped off in first Y% of videos"
```
**Why it matters:** Identifies specific improvement area

**Where to find:** Section 9 (Engagement Drop-off)

**Example:** "42% drop-off in first 25% led to recommendation for stronger opening hooks"

---

### 8. **Regional Performance Delta**
```
"Region A showed X times higher engagement with content type Y"
```
**Why it matters:** Demonstrates audience segmentation insight

**Where to find:** Section 3 (Regional Performance)

**Example:** "APAC: 2.1x higher engagement with market commentary vs. culture content"

---

### 9. **Stale Content Volume**
```
"Identified X videos not viewed in Y+ days"
```
**Why it matters:** Content lifecycle management, efficiency

**Where to find:** Section 7 (Archive Strategy)

**Example:** "Identified 47 videos (2.3TB) not viewed in 180+ days for archival"

---

### 10. **Watch Time**
```
"Tracked X hours of employee video engagement"
```
**Why it matters:** Shows impact on employee time/attention

**Where to find:** Section 0 (Setup) or Section 10 (ROI Summary)

**Example:** "Tracked 15,000+ hours of employee engagement with video content"

## Final Tips

1. **Lead with impact, follow with scale**
   - "Increased completion by 45%" (impact)
   - "...across 5,000 training videos" (scale)

2. **Use the "Rule of 3"**
   - Pick 3 best metrics
   - Use 3 examples in each STAR response
   - Practice 3 different STAR stories

3. **Connect to business value**
   - Every metric should answer "So what?"
   - Link to cost, risk, efficiency, or growth

4. **Be ready to go deeper**
   - Interviewer: "How did you calculate that?"
   - You: Have the methodology ready (not the SQL!)
